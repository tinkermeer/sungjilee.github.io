Long-Lasting Ephemera

Reminiscing about the past brings a mixture of happiness and sadness. There is comfort in the memories, yet also sorrow in knowing those moments are gone forever. Distance from my loved ones gave rise to a profound longing for connection. Over time, however, I came to realize that my existence no longer moved continuously within the rhythm of their lives. Years of separation formed an invisible yet unbearable barrier between us.
Driven by this bittersweet awareness, I seek to reclaim fleeting moments from the past. Using needle and thread, I stitch into faded photographs to bridge the void between memory and the present. The act of sewing becomes both restoration and meditation—a way of retrieving memories and transforming them into a renewed visual language.
